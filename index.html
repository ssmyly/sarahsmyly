About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Emory University, specializing in health economics and Industrial Organization. My research is focused on the intersection of these fields, particularly in structural health economics.

Before my doctoral studies, I earned a BS in Economics and a BA in Music from Berry College. My capstone project there is currently being developed into a research paper in collaboration with a former professor.

I also served as a writing tutor at Berry College, helping students at all stages of the writing process. I am honored to be a recipient of the Laney Graduate School Fellowship at Emory University, which supports my ongoing research. Outside of my academic work, I am an avid pianist and trumpet player.
