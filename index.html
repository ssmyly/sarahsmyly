About Me

Sarah Smyly is a second-year Ph.D. student at Emory University. Her current research interests lie in Applied Health Economics and Industrial Organization topics in health. She obtained a BS in Economics and a BA in Music at Berry College, where she engaged in research in Economics in a directed study regarding economics of education and, later, through a capstone project (now a working paper) relating to economics and politics. She also gained teaching experience as a tutor at the school's Writing Center. Sarah is honored to be a recipient of the Laney Graduate School Fellowship at Emory University, which helps to support her continuing studies and research.
